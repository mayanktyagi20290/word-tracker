How SEOTriggers Works

The moment you paste text, SEOTriggers tokenizes it, filters out common stop words, and builds a complete picture of your content. Here is what happens behind the scenes:

  • Tokenization & cleaning — your text is split into words, punctuation is stripped, and 200+ English stop words are optionally removed.
  • Frequency & density — every keyword is counted and its density calculated as a percentage of total words.
  • N-gram extraction — a sliding window finds repeating two to six word phrases for long-tail discovery.
  • Entities, intent & readability — pattern matching detects entities, classifies search intent and scores readability with the Flesch-Kincaid formula.
  • SEO scoring — all signals are combined into a single weighted score out of 100.

Everything updates live as you edit, so you can watch your SEO score climb while you refine your draft.

Word Frequency & Keyword Density Analyzer

The word frequency analyzer ranks every keyword by how often it appears and shows its density as a percentage. This is the heart of any keyword density checker. A primary keyword that sits around 1 to 2 percent usually reads naturally and signals relevance without overdoing it. Once a term climbs past roughly 3 percent it can look overused, and beyond 5 percent it starts to resemble keyword stuffing — something search engines actively penalize.

SEOTriggers color-codes these thresholds for you and surfaces overused and stuffed terms automatically, so you can rebalance distribution before publishing. The result is content that targets your keywords clearly while still reading like it was written for humans.

Long-Tail Keyword Finder

Long-tail keywords are specific phrases of three or more words that carry clear intent and face less competition than broad head terms. They are where most realistic ranking opportunities live. SEOTriggers detects repeating two to six word phrases across your content and ranks them by frequency, with a custom length option for longer phrases.

Reviewing your long-tail profile tells you which specific topics your content is actually emphasizing — and whether that matches the queries you want to rank for. If the phrases that appear most are not the ones you are targeting, that is a signal to refocus your draft.

Search Intent Analyzer

Matching content to search intent is one of the strongest, most underused ranking levers. SEOTriggers classifies your content into four intent types:

  • Informational — the reader wants to learn. Optimize for clarity, FAQs and comprehensive coverage.
  • Commercial — the reader is comparing options. Add comparisons, reviews and clear differentiators.
  • Transactional — the reader is ready to act. Strengthen calls to action and trust signals.
  • Navigational — the reader wants a specific page or brand.

The analyzer shows a confidence breakdown across all four so you can confirm your content aligns with the intent behind your target keyword.

NLP Entity Analyzer

Modern search engines understand topics through entities — the people, places, brands, products and concepts a page discusses. A page that mentions the right related entities signals genuine topical depth. SEOTriggers extracts locations, roles, industries and brand-style proper nouns from your content, groups them into topic clusters, and suggests semantically related (LSI) keywords.

Use this to spot coverage gaps: if an authoritative article on your topic would naturally mention certain entities and yours does not, adding them strengthens your relevance.

Competitor Gap Analysis

Ranking is competitive, so it helps to know exactly what the pages above you cover that you do not. With competitor gap analysis, you paste a competitor's content next to your own and SEOTriggers highlights the keywords, phrases and WH questions they include that are missing from your draft. Each gap is a concrete, prioritized opportunity to make your page more complete and competitive — without guesswork.

How to Write Content That Ranks: A Quick Checklist

A high SEO score is not about tricking search engines — it is about covering a topic thoroughly and clearly. Use this checklist alongside SEOTriggers to turn a draft into a page that earns rankings:

  • Lead with intent. Decide whether your target keyword is informational, commercial or transactional, then structure the page to satisfy that intent first.
  • Keep keyword density natural. Aim for a primary keyword around 1 to 2 percent and let synonyms and related terms carry the rest. If SEOTriggers flags a word above 3 percent, rephrase.
  • Build out long-tail coverage. Answer the specific sub-questions your readers actually search for. Repeated, relevant long-tail phrases widen the range of queries you can rank for.
  • Add entities and depth. Mention the people, tools, places and concepts a knowledgeable writer would naturally include. Topical completeness beats raw word count.
  • Demonstrate EEAT. Cite sources, add statistics, reference experts, and include first-hand experience. These trust signals matter most for health, finance and legal topics.
  • Structure for readers. Use clear headings, short paragraphs and varied sentence lengths. Good structure improves both readability scores and how easily Google parses your page.
  • Link internally. Use descriptive anchor text to connect related pages, helping search engines understand your site and keeping readers engaged.

Run your draft through SEOTriggers, fix the lowest-scoring factor it flags, and repeat. A few focused passes will move your score into the green and, more importantly, make your content genuinely more useful to the people you are writing for.
