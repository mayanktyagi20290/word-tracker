Frequently asked questions

  

    

      
Will blocking AI training crawlers hurt my Google rankings?▾

      
No. GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and Google-Extended are separate from Googlebot and Bingbot, which handle regular search indexing. Blocking AI training crawlers has no effect on your position in Google or Bing search results.

    

    

      
What is the difference between blocking a training bot and a search bot?▾

      
Training bots like GPTBot and ClaudeBot bulk-collect content to improve future model versions; blocking them is a content-rights decision with no effect on visibility. Search and retrieval bots like OAI-SearchBot and PerplexityBot build the index that AI answers cite from right now; blocking them removes your content from that engine's AI-generated answers immediately.

    

    

      
Does robots.txt actually stop AI crawlers from accessing my content?▾

      
For crawlers that honor robots.txt — which most major AI companies say they do — yes. But it's an honor system, not enforcement. Bytespider and some stealth crawlers have documented histories of ignoring robots.txt, so blocking those reliably requires a server or CDN/WAF-level rule in addition to robots.txt.

    

    

      
Should I block all AI crawlers by default?▾

      
Usually not. Blocking search and user-triggered crawlers like OAI-SearchBot, PerplexityBot, and ChatGPT-User removes you entirely from AI-generated answers and citations, which is the fastest-growing discovery channel for many sites. Most site owners want to block only pure training crawlers, if anything.

    

    

      
Do I need to update this robots.txt block regularly?▾

      
Yes. New AI crawlers appear a few times a year, and vendors occasionally rename or split existing ones. Review your AI crawler rules roughly quarterly to make sure the list still matches current vendor documentation.
