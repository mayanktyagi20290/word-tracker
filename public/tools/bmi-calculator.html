FAQ

What is a healthy BMI for adults?
For adults 20 and older, a BMI between 18.5 and 24.9 is considered healthy weight by WHO. Below 18.5 is underweight, 25–29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is classified as obese (Class I, II, or III depending on severity).
Is BMI accurate for women and men differently?
BMI uses the same scale for both sexes but women naturally carry more fat than men at the same BMI. This calculator provides a sex-adjusted body fat estimate using the Deurenberg formula (Body fat % = 1.2 × BMI + 0.23 × Age − 10.8 × Sex − 5.4, where Sex = 1 for male, 0 for female), which gives a more meaningful picture.
What is BMI Prime?
BMI Prime is your BMI divided by 25 — the upper limit of the normal range. A value of exactly 1.0 means you are right at the edge of normal weight. Below 1.0 is healthy or underweight; above 1.0 means overweight or obese. It lets you instantly see how far from the normal limit you are, in relative terms.
How is ideal body weight calculated?
Four clinical formulas are shown: Devine (most widely used clinically), Robinson, Miller, and Hamwi. Each uses height in inches and adjusts for sex. They differ slightly — Hamwi tends to give higher values for tall individuals, while Miller gives slightly lower ones. Using all four gives a realistic target range rather than a single number.
⚠️ Medical disclaimer: This calculator provides general health information only. BMI has limitations — it does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Consult a licensed healthcare provider before making any health or dietary decisions.
