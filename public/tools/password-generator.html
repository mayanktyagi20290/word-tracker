How to create a strong password

A strong password is long and unpredictable. Aim for at least 12–16 characters and mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers and symbols — length matters most, because each extra character multiplies the number of possible combinations. Use a different password for every account, and store them in a password manager rather than reusing or writing them down.

Why generate passwords in the browser?

This generator uses your browser's cryptographically secure random number generator and runs entirely on your device. The password never travels over the internet, isn't saved on any server, and disappears when you close the page — so it's safe to generate even highly sensitive credentials here.

Frequently asked questions

How do I generate a strong password?

Set the length to 12 or more, keep all character types enabled, and click Generate. Longer passwords with symbols are much harder to crack.

How long should a password be?

At least 12 characters for everyday accounts and 16 or more for important ones like email and banking. This tool supports up to 64 characters.

Are the generated passwords random and safe?

Yes. They use the Web Crypto secure random generator, not predictable math, and are created only in your browser — never transmitted or stored.

Should I reuse passwords?

No. Use a unique password for each account so that one breach can't compromise the others, and keep them in a password manager.
