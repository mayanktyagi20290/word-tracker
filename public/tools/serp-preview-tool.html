Why your snippet doesn't always match what you wrote

Google assembles the visible snippet dynamically — this preview shows your intended version, which is the best starting point for optimizing it.

FactorWhat it means for your snippet
Pixel width, not character countGoogle truncates based on rendered pixel width. Wide characters like "W" or "M" eat the limit faster than "i" or "l" — this tool estimates that width rather than just counting letters.
Desktop vs. mobileMobile results render in a narrower column, so the same title can truncate on mobile even if it fits fine on desktop.
Query-based boldingGoogle bolds terms that match what the searcher actually typed — it varies per search, so this preview is a representative simulation, not a guarantee.
Dynamic rewritingIf your title or description is missing, vague, or doesn't match search intent well, Google may substitute its own text pulled from the page or other sources.
