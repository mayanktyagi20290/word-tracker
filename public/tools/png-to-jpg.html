How to convert PNG to JPG

Drop one or more PNG files onto the box (or click to browse). Set the JPG quality, and each image is converted instantly. Transparent areas in a PNG become white in a JPG, since JPG doesn't support transparency. Use Download all for the whole batch.

Why convert PNG to JPG?

JPG files are usually much smaller than PNG for photographs, which makes pages load faster and fits upload-size limits. The trade-off is that JPG is lossy and has no transparency. Lower the quality slider for smaller files, or keep it near 90% for near-identical results.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert PNG to JPG?

Drop your PNG files onto the box above, choose a quality level, and each image is converted to JPG in your browser, ready to download individually or all at once.

What happens to a transparent PNG background?

JPG does not support transparency, so any transparent area is filled with white in the converted JPG. If you need transparency, keep the file as PNG.

Does converting reduce quality?

JPG is lossy, so there is a small quality trade-off for a much smaller file. At 90% quality the difference is usually invisible; lower it for smaller files.

Are my images uploaded to a server?

No. Conversion runs entirely in your browser, so your images never leave your device — faster and more private than tools that upload your files.

Can I convert many PNGs at once?

Yes. Drop multiple files, convert them together, and use Download all. There's no limit and no signup.
