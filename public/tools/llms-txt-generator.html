Frequently asked questions

  

    

      
What is llms.txt?▾

      
llms.txt is a plain-text Markdown file placed at your site's root (yoursite.com/llms.txt) that gives AI systems like ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity a clean, curated summary of your site and links to its most important resources, without the HTML, navigation, and ads a normal page contains.

    

    

      
Where does robots.txt end and llms.txt begin?▾

      
Robots.txt tells crawlers what they are and are not allowed to request. llms.txt does the opposite job: it tells AI systems what your site is about and points them to the content you most want them to read. One restricts access, the other provides curated signal.

    

    

      
Is llms.txt an official web standard?▾

      
Not yet in the way robots.txt or sitemap.xml are. It was proposed by Jeremy Howard of Answer.AI in September 2024 and has seen adoption from companies like Anthropic, Mintlify, and Vercel, but it isn't governed by a body like the IETF or W3C, and not every AI system commits to reading it.

    

    

      
Can I import my existing llms.txt to edit it here?▾

      
Yes. Paste your current file into the Import tab and this tool will parse it back into the name, summary, context, and sections fields so you can edit and regenerate it without starting from scratch.

    

    

      
What should I avoid putting in my llms.txt?▾

      
Avoid marketing language like "revolutionary" or "industry-leading" — factual, specific descriptions are more useful to a model than promotional phrasing. Also avoid linking to pages that are noindexed or blocked in robots.txt, since that sends a contradictory signal.
