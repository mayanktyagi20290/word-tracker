How to calculate your age

Pick your date of birth. Your age is calculated against today's date, which fills in automatically — but you can change the second date to find your age on any past or future day, such as a specific exam or eligibility date. The result shows your exact age as years, months and days, then the same span expressed as total months, weeks, days, hours and minutes.

How the calculation works

The calculator counts whole years from your birthday, then whole months, then the remaining days — handling leap years and months of different lengths correctly, so a birthday on the 31st or on 29 February is measured accurately. The totals are simply that same time span converted into other units.

Frequently asked questions

How do I calculate my exact age?

Enter your date of birth above. The tool instantly shows your age in years, months and days against today's date.

Does it update to today's date automatically?

Yes. The "Age at the date of" field is set to today when the page loads, so you just enter your birth date. You can change it to any date you like.

How is age counted in months and days?

It counts complete years first, then complete months, then leftover days, accounting for leap years and varying month lengths — the same way ages are calculated officially.

Is anything sent to a server?

No. The calculation runs entirely in your browser; no dates are uploaded or stored.
