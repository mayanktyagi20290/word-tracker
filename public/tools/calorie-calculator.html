What is BMR and TDEE?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at complete rest — just to keep your organs functioning. It accounts for about 60–75% of total daily calorie expenditure.

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is BMR multiplied by an activity factor. It represents the total calories you burn in a day including exercise and daily movement. This is the number you should eat to maintain your current weight.

Mifflin-St Jeor equation (used by this calculator)

This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, which is considered the most accurate BMR formula for most people:

Men:   BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age + 5
Women: BMR = 10 × weight(kg) + 6.25 × height(cm) − 5 × age − 161

TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor
  Sedentary:    × 1.2
  Light:        × 1.375
  Moderate:     × 1.55
  Active:       × 1.725
  Very Active:  × 1.9

Frequently asked questions

How many calories should I eat per day?▾
It depends on your age, gender, height, weight and activity level. The calculator above gives a personalised estimate. For most adults, maintenance calories range from 1,600–3,000 kcal/day. Enter your details above to get your specific number.
How do I calculate calorie deficit?▾
Subtract your target calories from your TDEE (maintenance). A 500 kcal/day deficit generally leads to losing approximately 0.5 kg (1 lb) per week, since 1 pound of fat ≈ 3,500 calories. A 1,000 kcal/day deficit would target about 1 kg per week. Going beyond 1,000 kcal deficit is generally not recommended.
How to calculate maintenance calories?▾
Calculate your BMR using the Mifflin-St Jeor equation, then multiply by your activity factor (1.2 to 1.9). The result is your TDEE — the calories you need to eat to maintain your current weight. Use the calculator above for an instant result.
How many calories should I eat to lose weight?▾
Eat 500–1,000 fewer calories than your TDEE. This creates a calorie deficit that leads to gradual weight loss of 0.5–1 kg per week. Losing more than 1 kg per week typically requires an aggressive deficit that can be difficult to sustain and may affect muscle mass.
What is a calorie deficit?▾
A calorie deficit occurs when you consume fewer calories than your body burns. Your body then uses stored fat (and some muscle) for energy, resulting in weight loss over time. A 500 kcal/day deficit = approximately 0.5 kg lost per week.
What is zigzag calorie cycling?▾
Zigzag cycling alternates between higher and lower calorie days while keeping the same weekly total. This can help prevent the body from adapting to a constant calorie level, which can slow weight loss. The weekly plan shown above uses your target calories but varies them ±250 kcal across the week.
